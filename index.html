How to use this calculator

How is liquidation price calculated?

For a long position: Liquidation Price = Entry × (1 − 1/Leverage + Maintenance Margin). Most exchanges use a maintenance margin of 0.5–1%. This calculator uses 0.5% as default. The actual value depends on your exchange — check their docs for precision.

What is funding rate and why does it matter?

Perpetual futures have no expiry, so they use a funding mechanism to keep price close to spot. Every 8 hours, longs pay shorts (or vice versa) a small fee. At 0.1%/8h, holding a $10,000 position for 24 hours costs $30 in funding. High positive funding means longs are crowded — a risk signal.

How do I size a position correctly?

The standard formula: Position Size = (Account × Risk%) / Stop Distance%. If your account is $1,000, you risk 2%, and your stop is 3% from entry, your position size is ($1,000 × 0.02) / 0.03 = $667. This calculator does this automatically based on your inputs.

What is a good risk/reward ratio for crypto perps?

Most professional traders target at least 1.5:1, with 2:1 or better being ideal. Anything below 1:1 means you need a win rate above 50% just to break even after fees — unsustainable for most strategies.

Does this work for Hyperliquid, Binance, Bybit?

Yes. The core calculations (position size, liquidation, R:R) are universal. Fee rates vary by exchange and account tier — adjust the fee field accordingly. Hyperliquid maker/taker fees are currently 0.01%/0.05%.
